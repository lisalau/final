Leaves of Three

Can you identify poison ivy?

About Toxicodendrons

Toxicodendron is a genus of flowering plants including poison ivy, poison oak, and poison sumac.

All members of the genus produce the skin-irritating oil urushiol, which can cause a severe allergic reaction.

Many hikers, gardeners, and nature enthusiasts remember to avoid these plants with the saying, 'Leaves of three, Let them be.' However, there are numerous look-alikes for each plant which are harmless.

Poison Ivy Look-alike

Duchesnea indica (Indian Strawberry plant)

At first glance, Indian Strawberry can be mistaken for poison ivy. It grows in leaves of three. And it is a creeper, which is often found low to the ground. But on closer inspection, the leaf shape is different.

Poison Oak Look-alike

Rubus ursinus (Wild Blackberry plant)

The leaves on wild blackberry plants appear in threes -- just like poison oak. But, upon closer inspection, notice that the stems have thorns.

Poison Sumac Look-alike

Rhus typhina (Staghorn Sumac)

Staghorn sumac has a very similar leaf shape and can be easily mistaken for poison sumac. But the staghorn's texture is more velvety. Its twigs have hair while poison sumac is hairless.
